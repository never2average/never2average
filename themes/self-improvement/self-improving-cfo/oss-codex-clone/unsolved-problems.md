# Unsolved Problems

The hardest technical problems were structured data and sandbox integration. Thin Wedge cannot just produce a nice explanation. It has to work with real financial context, local state, generated artifacts, and safe execution boundaries. Without that structure, it becomes another chat wrapper.

The current product blockers are adoption and proof of usefulness. The goal is not to convince a finance department. The goal is to help around 100 solo product operators run their businesses without needing a financial controller.

That makes the open problems concrete:

- Can a solo operator install it quickly enough that setup does not become the product?
- Can it make token budgets and forecasts legible without requiring a controller?
- Can it handle structured data and sandboxed workflows well enough that the output is trusted?
- Can it become useful often enough that adoption spreads through operators, not finance teams?

Thin Wedge works when a technical product builder can use it as their finance operator, not as a demo, but as part of how they decide what to spend, what to test, and what to stop.

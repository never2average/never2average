# Self-Improvement

Self-improvement is the only thing that is truly personal. My constraints are mine and my cost function is mine, so no one else can define what "better" means for me. This is where I believe AI finally empowers people instead of competing with them: I decide what the right thing is, and the agent works toward it without tiring. I am building a harness that makes this real, an agent that improves the components of my own world against a metric I set.

The personal part is not only ambition. It is also annoyance. I do not want to keep managing every loop by hand. Search quality, security posture, finance, token spend, model trajectory, and agent behavior all demand constant attention. Smart people still need context, monitoring, direction, feedback, and a roadmap. These projects are attempts to encode the judgment I would otherwise have to keep repeating.

The goal is not to remove intent. I keep the intent. The system should remove the repeated management work around it.

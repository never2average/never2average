# AI Researcher Intern, Immaculate v1

Research has always been rationed by compute. The best ideas die not because they were wrong, but because no one could afford to test them. [Immaculate](../overview.md) exists to end that: an agent that learns taste, the token efficiency of reaching the right answer, so that good ideas get their shot. v1 is shipped and breathing, and it needs someone to live in it.

I want a scrappy, obsessive researcher who will use Immaculate the way you would spend your last hour of GPU time, with intent and impatience. You will run real workloads through it, find where its taste is wrong, and turn that into the signal that makes the next version sharper. If you have trained models, fought a ranking or retrieval problem, or just cannot stop tuning things until they are right, this is for you.

## What you will do
- Push Immaculate hard, every day, on real research and search problems.
- Show me exactly where it wastes tokens and which hypotheses it should have killed.
- Build the feedback loop that compounds its taste.

## You are
- Opinionated about what a good experiment is, and able to say why.
- Hands-on with ML, search, or retrieval, and happy in a raw, early product.

## The deal

Paid stipend. Part-time and flexible around your life. Remote, global. If you are exceptional, this converts to a full-time or founding role.

Open an [issue](../../../../issues) and pitch me: who you are, and why this is yours. No forms.

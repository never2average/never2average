# Unsolved Problems

The unresolved Mynt problem is how to make financial product assembly feel simple without pretending money is simple. The product needs to preserve user intent, surface the hidden assumptions, and keep the resulting logic governable.

Open problems:

- How to decide when a natural-language request is specific enough to become financial logic.
- How to handle regulation, suitability, and jurisdiction without turning the product into a legal questionnaire.
- How to simulate or test proposed financial behavior before anything goes live.
- How to version product logic so changes are explainable and reversible.
- How to monitor live product behavior against the original user intent.

Mynt succeeds if it lets a user describe a financial outcome and arrive at a governed product configuration, with the important assumptions visible and the dangerous steps gated.

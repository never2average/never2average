# Learnings

The first learning is that Relay cannot stay a one-time guide if it succeeds. Every Relay user who gets far enough will eventually need Cabinet, because the company stops being a playbook and becomes a living system.

The second learning is that durable company state needs two shapes. It needs a database because agents need fast, current, agent-readable state. It also needs a Git repo because the company needs a versioned record of why things changed. Plans, spend limits, roadmap branches, agent responsibilities, eval results, and decisions should not disappear into chat.

The third learning is that the hard parts are not the UI. The hard parts are agent-readable company state, eval wiring, human-agent handoffs, persistent memory, and permissions. A pretty interface is not the company runtime. The runtime is whether the agents can collaborate without losing authority, context, or measurement.

The fourth learning is that Cabinet should make the founder feel less like they are manually running the second company. The founder can still set direction, constraints, customer focus, spend limits, and the type of people they want to work with. Cabinet should turn that into agent work and roadmap movement.

The fifth learning is that the company must still have one accountable CEO. Cabinet can coordinate a cabinet of agents, but the founder remains the final authority unless they explicitly delegate a narrow responsibility.

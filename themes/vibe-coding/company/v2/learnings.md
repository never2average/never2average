# Learnings

The first learning is that a company operating system needs memory more than it needs more chat. The product has to remember what the founder wanted, what was tried, what worked, what failed, and which assumptions are now part of the company.

The second learning is that permissions and ownership matter early. If agents can change company state, the system needs to know who owns a decision, who can approve it, and what evidence was used.

The third learning is that dashboards are often a symptom of weak agency. The goal is not to display every metric; the goal is to let language create and adjust the operating loop that uses those metrics.

# Unsolved Problems

The unresolved Cabinet problem is how to make company state agent-readable without freezing the company into rigid software. A real company changes constantly, and the operating system has to adapt without becoming vague.

Open problems:

- How to represent company state so agents can act on it without hallucinating authority.
- How to design permissions for agent-run work without burying every action in approvals.
- How to make artifacts first-class: plans, reports, launches, financial decisions, hiring loops, and customer research.
- How to let the founder steer through language while preserving enough structure for repeatability.
- How to measure whether the operating system is actually making the company smarter, not just busier.

Cabinet is successful when the founder can state an objective and watch the company runtime assemble the right people, agents, workflows, and evidence around it.
